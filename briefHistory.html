Historical Perspective

The Makati City Library was originally named the Makati Public Library which was established by Resolution No. 22 and Ordinance No. 9 of the Special Meeting of the Municipal Council of Makati, Rizal, held on January 28, 1954. It was organized in 1954 by the Makati Women's League, whose advisers were the late Mrs. Dolores M. Rivera (ex-councilor) and late Mrs. Mercedes Samson.

The Library was turned over to the Municipal Government shortly after its opening. Miss Veronidia Gutierrez Garcia (now Mrs. Veronidia Garcia Garcia) was then appointed as library custodian. The library was located in a small room at the back of the Makati Fire Department. It had sparse furniture consisting of a long plywood table, two long benches, an office table for the librarian, and bookshelves enough to contain the 300 volumes of books that the library acquired as initial stock from the Bureau of Public Libraries, now the National Library of the Philippines. Another 500 volumes of books were later acquired from the defunct House of Representatives at the instance of Mrs. Dolores M. Rivera. Some civic-spirited citizens subscribed to two newspapers for the library. An average of 100 persons used to come daily to the library despite the scarcity of reading materials, most of them students and out-of-school youths. As the years passed, some changes in the library took place. But these were mostly insignificant because, with the election of a new Mayor, new personnel managed the library, and its location was also changed.

In 1963, the library was moved to the 4th floor of the new municipal building during the term of the late Mayor Maximo Estrella. The mayor launched a program upgrading all departments of the municipal government and the library began an important phase of development with the purchasing of facilities including the purchase of new books. When typhoon Yoling partially destroyed the building in 1967, the library was moved to the 2nd floor of the old municipal building. The place was spacious enabling the library personnel to put up the various sections. It was also then, that the library expanded with two branches in Barrio Bangkal and Barrio Comembo. The library and its two branches had at one time earned the distinction of being the Most Outstanding Library in the Philippines under the leadership of the Municipal Librarian, Mrs. Veronidia Garcia Garcia.

When the late Nemesio I. Yabut assumed office in 1972, one of his first moves was the relocation of the library to the second floor of the Eassale Building at J. P. Rizal Avenue corner Makati Avenue.

In 1975, the library was able to have a Bookmobile library donated by the USAID. This Bookmobile was the first of its kind in Metro Manila. It was able to serve different schools and some far barrios of Makati. In the latter part of 1981, the bookmobile unit had mechanical trouble that caused it to stop operations. In time for the 1984 - 1985 school year, the Ayala Corporation on the occasion of its 150th Anniversary donated 749 books on different subjects and different school levels. The Ayala Corporation also paid for the repair of the office equipment.

The library established and opened up five barangay libraries and five rolling micro libraries all over Makati. However, the latter were operational for one year only.

But neither the founders of this public library were discouraged by the odds; lack of financial support, lack of books, the seemingly wait-and-see attitude of the public and the municipal government. These insurmountable obstacles proved to be the catalyst that further motivated the founders to ignore the hindrances and keep moving forward to achieve their goals. Thus, give the people of Makati a lasting legacy, a public library of its own. This dream was fulfilled, but still, the library had no permanent building it could call its own. Under the administration of Mayor Jejomar C. Binay, the library was moved to another building on June 6, 1987, with bigger space and better facilities.

The present efforts of the Library staff are geared towards updating the collection of books and other reading materials to cope with the advancement in science and modern times and the delivery of better public library services to the people of Makati.

Other institutions that were giving continued support to update the library collections were The Asia Foundation, Inc., Makati Rotary Club, Thomas Jefferson Library, International School, Makati Jaycees, and other civic-minded citizens of Makati. Among their donations were books and audio-visual materials. Another institution is the National Library whose support is not only in terms of yearly allocation of books but also of supervision in the efficient management and functioning of the library.

Shortly, after its establishment in 1954 until the 1990's, the Makati Municipal Library was under the Mayor's Office. After the cityhood of Makati in 1995, the library's name was changed to Makati City Library under the administration of former Mayor Jejomar C. Binay. The reorganizational structure of the city then placed the Library under the Education Department as one of its divisions.

In February 2000, the Makati City Library was relocated to the 8th Floor of the New City Hall Building under its new City Librarian, Mrs. Rosario Ibay Amerca. The facilities were much better with mobile shelves provision, computers with internet service, new tables, carrels, chairs and an audio-visual room. The ambiance in the new library is more conducive to serious reading and research. With the support of the City Government, The National Library, and other stakeholders the City Library continued to evolve to become a hybrid library.
