Introduction

Makati City Library plays a vital role in building the community. As an active partner in education, it supports the educational vision of the City Mayor for Makatizens by providing access to educational research materials required by in-school and out-of-school individuals. It serves also as a repository of intellectual and cultural creations of the Filipino people, and shares in the propagation and dissemination of the historical and cultural heritage. The promotion of nationalism and reading interest through exhibits, displays, and programs is a continuing commitment.

Overview of the City Library System

The Makati City Library is one of the educational, historical, and cultural agencies of the City Government of Makati under the supervision of the National Library with regards to its function and administratively, under the Education Department. The functions, goals, and objectives of this agency are all directed toward the fulfillment of its users' needs. Service-oriented, the City Library preserves and develops its facilities, collection, and other library resources to meet the information needs of researchers, students, out-of-school youth, persons with disability, senior citizens, and the general reading public. The ultimate goal, therefore, is to assist man in his search for Truth and Wisdom.


The Makati City Library has a wide array of library materials comprised of general reference, gender and development, materials for persons with disability, Filipiniana and local history, law and governance, periodicals and serials, non-book materials, government publications and electronic resources.

Services Offered

  • Book Lending Services → Library clientele can borrow books and reading materials for home use provided they are holders of Makati City Library I.D. A maximum of three books for seven days is the duration of the circulation.
  • Query Services Through Email and Phone → The librarian entertains researchers through telephone or email inquiries.
  • Library Awareness Program→ The City Library holds different cultural and educational activities during the Library and Information Services Month, National Book Week, National Children's Book Day, and Public Library Day celebrations.
  • Free Internet and Wifi → Free internet services are allowed exclusively for research purposes.
  • Tekno-Aklatan → Tekno-aklatan is an e-resource subscribed to and downloaded by the National Library that can be used without the Internet. It is part of the package of Koha Integrated Library System installed in the library computers.
  • Online Public Access Catalog → The online public access catalog is a database of Makati City Library's book collection to help clients easily search and retrieve the library materials they need.
  • Practicum and On-The-Job Training → The City Library accepts students from different schools for on-the-job training in all phases of library work.
  • Special Corner for Disabled Persons →The National Council for the Welfare of Disabled Persons and Makati's Department of Social Welfare have coordinated with the library to set up a special corner for disabled persons. The NCWDP has provided the reading materials for this special reading corner and the National Library has donated some Braille books as well.
  • Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas Corner → In coordination with the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas - Economic and Financial Learning Center, the library was provided with the BSP's publications on economics and finance for public use.
  • Makati Local History Unit → Makati Local History collection is the library's special and unique library materials dealing with the historical and cultural heritage of the City. As a public library, local history materials should be given paramount importance as they reflect Makatizens' values, aspirations, traditions, culture, history, and development.

Required Documents for Securing Library ID

  • Application Form
  • Signature of Guarantors
  • Any Government Issued ID/School ID

Applying for Library ID

  • Step 1: Secure application form in the Circulation Section.
  • Step 2: Fill up the form and let the guarantor sign at the designated column.
  • Step 3: Submit to the Circulation Section In-Charge the duly accomplished form.
  • Step 4: Get the library card one to two days after submission of the application form.

Rules and Regulations Inside Makati City Library

  • The library is open Monday to Friday from 8:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. NO NOON BREAK.
  • Only residents of Makati City Library ID issued by the Makati City Library are allowed to borrow and bring home books and other reading materials.
  • Non-Makati residents may also avail of the library's books and other reading materials but to be read-only inside the library.
  • A borrower may bring home a maximum of three (3) books to be returned after seven days.
    The following materials may be borrowed for short duration only (overnight for the minimum period and two (2) days for the maximum period):
    a. New issues of newspapers and magazines
    b. Filipiniana books
    c. Reference books (subject to the discretion of the person in charge of the section).
  • Any book that gets damaged while in the custody of the borrower should be replaced by him/her with a book of the same title and quality of print or with any book of the same subject content, the price of which should not be less than that of the damaged book.
  • Notice will be sent to the borrower who failed to return the book/s on the due date. Penalty will be charged for books not returned on time by the number of days that books were not returned after the due date.
  • Eating, smoking, and making unnecessary noise are prohibited inside the library. Cellphone owner should place their cellphone on vibrator alert or low volume.
  • Free internet service is allowed exclusively for research purposes.
  • Laptop and desktop computers can be used for online training, and other educational purposes, provided that the user will put on a headset to minimize noise. The client should log in with the designated number of laptops, desktops, and accessories on the logbook for monitoring purposes.
  • Bringing of personal gadgets, devices, and laptop computers is allowed but charging them in the electrical outlet of the library is prohibited. Clients should bring their power bank.
  • Children aged five years old and above can be accommodated in the Children's Unit. However, those younger than four should be accompanied by their guardians.
  • Makati City Library is accepting book donations in compliance with its donation policy. Books that are outdated, obsolete, and in poor physical condition are not acceptable. The librarian will evaluate and assess which donated books will be added to the collection or be weeded out and disposed of.
